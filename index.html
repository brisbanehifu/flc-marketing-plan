A. "Blue Rock" Shale a Metre Below — SEQ Mini-Diggers Bouncing Off

SURPRISE COSTS / GEOLOGY Pool & site-cut buyers across South East Queensland keep hitting "blue rock" shale within a metre of the surface. Standard mini-excavators literally bounce off it. Homeowners discover this AFTER the cheap quote is signed — needing hydraulic hammers and rippers means a fresh invoice. This is the dominant new-build cost shock in SEQ in 2026.

"In many parts of South East Queensland, you hit 'blue rock' or heavy shale just a metre below the surface, and standard mini-diggers often bounce off this material. Specialised hydraulic hammers and rippers are needed to break through without sending excessive vibrations through your home's foundations."
Source: Plunge Pools Brisbane 2026 Price Guide · Norfolk Pools Brisbane Cost Guide 2026
FLC EDGE: Lead a content series titled "Brisbane's Hidden Layer." Post a 30-sec video of an FLC ripper hitting blue shale at 800mm in Caboolture/North Lakes. Disclose the rock-allowance line item upfront on every quote — refundable if untouched.

CRITICAL The single most-discussed frustration in Brisbane homeowner forums. Contractors arrive for a site visit, promise a quote within days, then vanish. No callback, no email, nothing.

"Sourcing tradies for small jobs in SE Queensland is a nightmare. Some even turn up, quote you, get agreement, then ghost you completely when you try to contact them."

FLC EDGE: Quote within 48 hours. Phone callback always. If FLC says they'll ring back, they ring back. Make this a public promise on every post.

2. "Rock Clause" Cost Blowouts

CRITICAL Builders quote a small "provisional amount" for site works that's never enough. Then rock is found and the homeowner is hit with $20K+ in variations. Soil tests can't reliably predict rock.

"Rock removal is generally charged at around $250 per cubic metre, which can equal $20,000 for the average pool if it was completely in rock."
Source: Build in Oz / Stroud Homes / Whirlpool building forum
FLC EDGE: Disclose rock allowance UPFRONT in every quote. Refundable if not encountered. Show the homeowner the calc. This builds trust faster than any other tactic.

3. Cowboy / Unlicensed Operators

CRITICAL Inexperienced earthmoving contractors causing damage to underground utilities, foundations, and neighbours' fences. Walls collapsing within months.

"There are a lot of cowboy landscapers out there without a licence and operating illegally. An inexperienced unqualified handyman could build a small retaining wall under $3,300 but if it's not properly constructed it could begin to lean and eventually collapse within a few short months."
Source: Concrete Sleeper Retaining Walls Brisbane / Brisbane City Council complaints data
FLC EDGE: Lead with QBCC Licence #76041 in every post and every quote. Master Builders membership. Public liability insurance disclosed. "Licence number on every invoice."

4. Hidden Site Costs Blowing Out Budgets

HIGH The #1 surprise for Queensland new home builders. Builders bury earthworks in vague "site costs TBC". Homeowners commit to a fixed price, then get crushed.

"Builders typically only include a small 'provisional amount' for site works, and it is never enough. It's not hard to rack up an extra $50K if you wanted everything finished."
Source: Davolyn Constructions / Whirlpool "advice needed new home hidden costs"
FLC EDGE: Itemised quotes — every line transparent. Site cut, soil disposal, rock allowance, retaining, trenching all priced separately. No "TBC" lines.

5. Underground Utility Strikes

HIGH Cowboy operators digging without DBYD checks, hitting water/sewer/gas lines and electrical. Massive fines, project delays, real injury risk.

"It is very common for homeowners to encounter underground utilities, and accidentally striking them can damage utilities, force the project on hold, lead to fines, and even cause serious injury or death."
Source: Ryno Hire / Master Builders Queensland — Excavation hazards guide
FLC EDGE: "Dial Before You Dig" check on every job, every time. Document it. Show the homeowner the DBYD report.

6. Retaining Wall Failures from Bad Drainage

HIGH Walls cracking, bulging, and collapsing because the contractor skipped weep holes, gravel backfill, or aggdrain. Brisbane's clay soils make this brutal.

"Inadequate drainage is one of the leading causes of retaining wall failure. When water accumulates without proper drainage, it creates excessive hydrostatic pressure, causing the wall to crack, bulge, or collapse."
Source: KG Landscape / Queensland Retaining Wall Lawyers
FLC EDGE: Every retaining wall job includes proper aggdrain, gravel backfill, geomesh, and weep holes — show photos of this in posts. Educate the market.

MEDIUM-HIGH Contractors making verbal "while we're here" upgrades, then billing for them. Massive QBCC and QCAT dispute volumes.

MEDIUM-HIGH Pool buyers caught out by tight access, rock, soil disposal, and crane requirements. $5-20K of surprise costs are common.

Template #5 — Retaining Wall Question

WHEN TO USE: Someone asking about retaining wall costs, failures, neighbour disputes, or council requirements.

Retaining walls are where most cowboy contractors get exposed. Tip #10 — When Council Approval Matters (And When It Doesn't)

BEST FOR: Anyone confused about what needs approval — retaining walls, fences, sheds, driveways.

Quick guide for Brisbane and Moreton Bay homeowners on what needs council approval and what doesn't, when it comes to earthworks and yard projects: ✅ DOES need approval: - Retaining walls OVER 1 metre high - Excavation OVER 1 metre below natural ground level - Building work over a sewer or close to a boundary easement - Anything affecting stormwater flow onto a neighbour - Most pool builds (handled by the pool company usually) ❌ DOESN'T need approval (usually): - Retaining walls UNDER 1 metre - Garden bed cuts - Standard driveway construction on your own block - Trenching for services within your own boundary - Site cut for a slab (handled under the building approval for the actual slab) ⚠️ The trap: even when you DON'T need council approval, you still need the work done properly. "No approval" doesn't mean "no engineering". A 0.9m retaining wall that fails is still your problem. If you're not sure whether your job needs approval, ring the council before you start.
